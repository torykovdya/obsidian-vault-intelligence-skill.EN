# Opportunity Patterns — From Notes to Output

Use this when running Phase 3 (Opportunity Intelligence) of a Vault Intelligence analysis.
Apply ALL 15 patterns — 5 per category. Report every match found in the vault.

---

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Articles

### Pattern 1: The Dense Cluster
**Signal**: 5+ notes on one topic, many interconnected
**Opportunity**: Long-form article or definitive guide — "Everything I know about X, organized into a framework"
**Angle**: You've done the synthesis work others haven't. Publish the map.
**Example**: 8 notes on AI agents → "A practical map of AI agent architectures"

### Pattern 2: The Surprising Connection
**Signal**: Two separate clusters that share a non-obvious link
**Opportunity**: "The X you didn't know was related to Y" piece — bridges two audiences
**Angle**: Insight that combines things others keep separate; rare by definition
**Example**: Notes on Zettelkasten + notes on AI training data → "Your note-taking system is secretly a training dataset"

### Pattern 3: The Contradiction
**Signal**: Two notes that disagree with each other (especially across time)
**Opportunity**: "I was wrong about X — here's what I learned" — highest-trust format in technical writing
**Angle**: Intellectual honesty + evolution of thinking = credibility
**Example**: Early note "prompting is just tricks" vs later "prompting is a cognitive interface"

### Pattern 4: The Unanswered Question
**Signal**: A question appears in notes but was never resolved; shows up across multiple notes
**Opportunity**: Research piece or open exploration essay — "I'm trying to figure out X, here's what I've found"
**Angle**: Intellectual honesty + reader becomes co-investigator
**Example**: Recurring question about "how do you evaluate agent reliability?" → research article

### Pattern 5: The Hard-Won Lesson
**Signal**: Notes from a project or experiment with mistakes explicitly documented
**Opportunity**: Case study or lessons-learned post — specificity makes it credible
**Angle**: "I did the thing so you don't have to" — saves readers real pain
**Example**: Notes from building a RAG pipeline → "What I broke building my first RAG system"

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Products

### Pattern 1: The Repeated Pain
**Signal**: Same frustration appears in 3+ different notes across different dates
**Opportunity**: Tool or service that eliminates that exact pain — you are your own first user
**Example**: Multiple notes complaining about "summarizing Slack threads manually" → Slack summarizer agent

### Pattern 2: The Manual Workflow
**Signal**: A multi-step process described in detail in notes — steps, tools, order
**Opportunity**: Automate or productize that workflow for others who have the same process
**Example**: Detailed notes on "how I research a topic" → packaged research agent or template

### Pattern 3: The Expertise Gap
**Signal**: You've gone deep on X, but the market teaches X poorly or not at all
**Opportunity**: Course, guide, or tool for people at the beginning of where you are now
**Example**: Deep notes on prompt engineering for non-coders → course for marketers

### Pattern 4: The Unique Combination
**Signal**: Your two clusters together are rare — most practitioners know one, not both
**Opportunity**: Niche product serving the intersection — where you have no direct competitors
**Example**: AI knowledge + healthcare domain → medical note-taking AI tool

### Pattern 5: The Framework
**Signal**: You've developed a personal system, taxonomy, or scoring model others don't have
**Opportunity**: Template, workbook, scorecard, or tool built on your framework
**Example**: Your personal "AI tool evaluation criteria" → public scorecard tool or newsletter series

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Automations

### Pattern 1: Repeated Copy-Paste
**Signal**: Notes describe doing the same operation manually across multiple tools
**Opportunity**: One-click sync or transformation between those exact tools
**Example**: "I take each meeting summary and put it in Notion" → auto-sync automation

### Pattern 2: Information Aggregation
**Signal**: Notes describe gathering data from multiple sources manually on a regular cadence
**Opportunity**: Scheduled agent that pulls, filters, and delivers the aggregated view
**Example**: "I check 5 AI newsletters every morning" → AI digest agent delivered to inbox

### Pattern 3: Content Repurposing
**Signal**: Notes describe transforming content between formats (blog → thread, transcript → summary)
**Opportunity**: Automated repurposing pipeline with format-specific prompts per output type
**Example**: Notes on "turning articles into tweets" → n8n workflow with Claude API

### Pattern 4: Quality Checks
**Signal**: Notes describe reviewing or auditing things on a recurring basis
**Opportunity**: Automated checker that runs on a schedule and flags issues proactively
**Example**: Notes on "checking if my prompts follow best practices" → prompt linter agent

### Pattern 5: Research Pipelines
**Signal**: Notes describe a consistent research process with defined steps and sources
**Opportunity**: Codify the process into a reusable agent or workflow others can run too
**Example**: Notes on "how I validate a business idea" → automated market research agent

## Trajectory Forecasting Logic

To forecast 3–6 months out, apply this logic in order:

1. **Identify the strongest cluster** — this is the user's primary expertise signal
2. **Find the fastest-growing cluster** — this is where investment is paying off
3. **Identify the key gap** — the missing piece that unlocks the next archetype level
4. **Project the combination** — what becomes possible when the gap is filled?
5. **Find the growth archetype below** — which pattern fits? Adjust forecast accordingly.

### Growth Archetypes (apply to Phase 4)

**The Deepener**: One dominant cluster, steadily adding depth and nuance
→ Forecast: Becomes the go-to resource for that specific niche; courses and consulting follow naturally

**The Connector**: Medium depth in several areas, notes frequently bridge between them
→ Forecast: Creates unique cross-domain products or frameworks; "the person who explains X to Y people"

**The Builder**: Strong technical depth + product/business awareness emerging
→ Forecast: Ships tools or SaaS at the intersection of expertise; productized services before that

**The Teacher**: Deep knowledge cluster + growing notes on communication, content, or audience
→ Forecast: Course, community, or newsletter business; the vault becomes the curriculum

Identify which archetype fits the vault and name it explicitly in Phase 4 output.
